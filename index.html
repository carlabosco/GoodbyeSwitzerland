1 YEAR


Moving to another country is a big deal, even more so when you have a family. You might be lucky if you have an entire year to plan your move, and it is certainly doable in a much tighter time frame, but planning ahead is a lot more comfortable, no question.


Knowing that I had only one more year in Switzerland allowed me to be more in touch with the country, more conscious of what I like and dislike about life here, and plan trips to destinations that I won’t be visiting in a long time. Living in the heart of Europe has its perks, so it’s good to take advantage of this while we can. Besides, knowing that this will be your last Chilbi makes the Apfel-Chüechli mit Vanillesauce even tastier.

COMPARISONS

If you are still considering whether or not it’s time to leave Switzerland, it might help to do some comparisons against other cities you have in mind. Now, while it’s hard to decide to move or not to move based on cold data alone - safe and clean don’t always mean happy - running a cost of life comparison on a website like Numbeo can certainly help. This is the case especially if you already have a job offer to go somewhere else and you want to know what your new salary will buy you there.
